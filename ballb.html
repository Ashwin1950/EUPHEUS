BA LLb

    The Bachelor of Arts - Bachelor of Legislative Law (BA LLB) course is an integrated law degree that the law aspirants can pursue right after qualifying their Class 12th examinations. The law degree is an integration of Arts and Law subjects and is of five-years duration. Therefore, as part of the BA LLB course, the candidates study subjects including Economics, History, Political Science, Sociology along with law subjects like Civil Law, Criminal Law, Labour Law, Tax Law, Administrative Law, Corporate Law, Patent Law etc. The course-curriculum of BA LLB degree is such that law, as well as, the arts subjects, are included in each semester. Moreover, candidates are taught about law case studies, moot courts, law internships, seminars and interactions with retired judges from High courts and the Supreme Court of India.

    Students who wish to become a lawyer and possess good communication skills along with a bent for science can opt for the program. After pursuing B.Sc + LLB, one can pursue a masters degree in law (LLM) as it makes you eligible for teaching jobs in reputed universities.

Course details

  • Full-Form: Bachelor of Arts + Bachelor of Legislative Law
  • Duration: 5 years
  • Eligibility: 10+2 with minimum 45% to 50% marks aggregate
  • Admission Process: Based on entrance examination
  • Top Entrance Exam: CLAT, MH CET Law, LSAT India, KIITEE Law, TS LAWCET etc.
  • Top Colleges: KIIT School of Law, Mahatma Jyoti Rao Phoole University, Amity University, OPJS University, NIMS University etc.
  • Average course fees: INR 2,00,000.
  • Job Options: Criminal Lawyer, Legal Advisor, Judge, Legal Journalist, Government Lawyer, etc.
  • Average Salary Package: INR 6,00,000 - 8,00,000
  • Area of Employment: Trilegal, Khaitan & CO, Talwar Thakore & Associates, S&R Associates, Luthra & Luthra Law Offices etc.
