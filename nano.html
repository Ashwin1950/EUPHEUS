NanoTechnology

    Nanotechnology is the study of applications and nanoscale objects that can be used across various science platforms such as Physics, Chemistry, Biology, engineering, and materials science.

It is a full-time, 4 years course.

Why NanoTechnology?

  • In the program the students will also be able to specialize in a certain area.

  • Nanoscience has the potential to reshape the world around us. It could lead to revolutionary breakthroughs in fields ranging from manufacturing to healthcare.

  • Researchers are developing nanostructured filters that can remove virus cells and other impurities from water, which may ultimately help create clean, affordable and abundant drinking water.

  • Students pursuing Nanotechnology will be studying to make the world a better place by designing more sustainable energy.

  • Curious and imaginative with a scientific frame of mind and a great deal of patience: the nanotechnology revolution will be fueled by years of painstaking research.

  • When you take a class on nanomaterials, you'll investigate their special properties. You'll learn how to measure and test them to find out, for example, how well they conduct electricity. You'll study both inorganic and organic materials and look at the interactions between them.
