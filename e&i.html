Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering

    Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering is a 4-year undergraduate course in the field of engineering which combines the study of electronics part for sensing the different instrumentation engineering.

It is a full-time, 4 years course.

Why Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering?

  • Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering graduates are recruited by a variety of industries in various domains such as Design, Development, Automation, Control, Fabrication, Inspection, Quality Control, Maintenance and Service and so on.

  • They can work in Process Industries (refineries, fertilizers, paper, power plant, food processing, chemical etc.), Process Automation (Emerson), Factory Automation (Hyundai). Besides, they can also go for fields like Avionics (Measurement and Stability), Automobile, Electronics, Biomedical etc.

  • The average salary range for a fresher in this field in India is INR 4-5 lakhs per annum which is proportional to experience and skill set.

  • Also, successful graduates can find career opportunities in an industry- based companies that manufacture and apply instrumentation equipment for power plants, VLSI and embedded systems, robotics, aerospace and various other automation industries.

Course Details

  • Course Level - Undergraduate Course
  • Full-Form- Bachelor of Technology in Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering
  • Duration -4 years
  • Eligibility- Candidates should have passed the 10+2 examination with at least 50% marks in science stream (PCM) from a recognized board.
  • Admission Process- Merit-Based/ Entrance Examination
  • Course Fee- INR 1,00,000 – INR 12,00,000
  • Average Salary INR 2,00,000 – 10,00,000
  • Top Recruiting Companies -BHEL, BEL, NTPC, ONGC, DRDO, ISRO, HAL, GE, L&T, Honeywell, Allen-Bradley, Cairn India, Siemens, Cognizant, Wipro, Accenture, Amazon, Infosys, Essar, ABB Group, Yokogawa Electric, Capgemini Cognizant, Deloitte, Tata Power, Tata Steel, Tech Mahindra, TCS, ThoughtWorks, SAP
  • Job Positions- Instrumentation Engineer, Electronics Engineer, Instrumentation and Control Technician, Electrical R & D Engineer, Automation Engineer, Mechanical Engineer, etc.
